Introduction¶

This Dataset collects data of over 100k medical appointments from Brazil and is focused on whether or not patients show up for their appointment. A number of characterstics about the patient are included

  • ScheduledDay: tells us on what day the patient set up their appointment.
  • Neighborhood: indicates the location of the hospital.
  • Scholarship: indicates whether or not the patient is enrolled in Brasilian welfare program Bolsa Famlia.
  • No Show: it says No if the patient showed up to their appointment, and Yes if they did not show up.



Questions that I want to explore on the dataset are:

  1. What is the percentage of patients who show up vs who do not show up for their appointment?
  2. What factors are important for us to know in order to predict if a patient will show up for their scheduled appointment?

#function to plot for the specified features
def plott(*args):
    a=df.groupby(args[0])[args[1]].count()
    b=df.groupby(args[0])[args[1]].mean()
    a=a[a.values>1000]
    a=a.to_frame('patients count')
    b=b.to_frame('mean')
    c=pd.merge(a,b,left_index=True,right_index=True)
    c.reset_index(level=0,inplace=True)
    c.plot.bar(args[0],'mean',color='steelblue')
    plt.ylabel('Mean')
    plt.title('Mean of patients who did not show for their appointment on factor {}'.format(args[0]))
In [37]:

We can clearly observe that difference in days between scheduled day and appointment day plays a key role. patients who scheduled their appointment on the same day are by far most likely to show up for their appointment than patients who scheduled their appointment on previous days. Also we can observe an irregular rising trend which is indicating that patients who scheduled their appointment so many days prior to the appointment day are less likely to show for their appointment.

Conclusions¶

Finally, summarizing the findings and the results that have been performed.

  • 20.2% patients do not for their appointments while 79.8% patients attend their appointment
  • 65% of patients who had their appointments are female
  • The important factor to decide the no show is their scheduled and appointment dates.
  • Patients who schedule their appointment on the same are more likely to show for their appointment
  • Sending SMS is not required as we can see better percentage of patients who attended their appointment did not receive sms
  • 28.9% patients of santos dumont neighbourhood are likely to not show up their appointment which is the highest
  • Old age patients are more likely to attend their appointment compared to other age group patients
  • It is observed that patients having scholarship are likely to miss their appointments than patients who do not have scholarship
  • We can observe that patients who have diabetes are less likely to miss their appointments
  • Factors like gender,alcoholic are not factors to decide whether patient will show or not show

Limitations¶

  • The scope of this analysis is limited to only data which is collected. Any Analysis or Trends cannot be induced to larger population or other data.
  • Analysis performed is to know the value distributions, causations cannot be drawn. In order to do it, Statistical tests need to be performed
